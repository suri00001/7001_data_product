Model note: Based on the marginal effect from the Country FE model (coefficient 0.237, p<0.001). R² within = 0.108 — aging explains roughly 11% of within-country variation. This estimate is a directional fiscal signal, not a precise prediction.

Data Sources
World Bank World Development Indicators: health expenditure (% of GDP), urban population, GDP per capita, hospital beds per 1,000 people. UN Population Division: population aged 65+ as share of total. Sample: 197 sovereign states, 2000–2022. N = 4,495 after listwise deletion. GDP 2022 reference values: World Bank national accounts data.
Methodology Note
Preferred model: Country Fixed Effects (linearmodels, Python). Standard errors clustered at the entity level. Two-way FE result attenuated due to global aging trend collinear with year fixed effects — a standard identification constraint, not a modelling error. Five-model robustness check confirms directional consistency. WQD7001 Group Assignment.
